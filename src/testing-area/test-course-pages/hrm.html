Module 1: Menu Planning and Pricing in Food Service Management

Module 1 Objectives and Overview

Module Objectives:

  • Learn to select the most suitable menu format for a specific food service operation, taking into account its unique characteristics and target audience
  • Explore the variables that play a crucial role in establishing menu prices, including factors such as food cost, demand, and profit margins
  • Develop the skills to assign prices to menu items based on a nuanced evaluation of their popularity, cost, and potential for profit, ensuring that your offerings align with revenue goals

Overview

Welcome to the first module of HRM 104: Hotel Food and Beverage Management! In this introductory session, we delve into the vital role of menus in the food service sector, equipping you with essential knowledge and skills for your future career. Menus are much more than a list of dishes; they are strategic tools that influence a myriad of operational aspects, from item selection and pricing to staff allocation. Together, we will explore various menu types and styles, unraveling how they facilitate effective cross-utilization of food and beverage resources, and examining the critical factors that guide menu pricing. Our journey will also encompass the art of balancing customer appeal with financial sustainability, ensuring that your menu not only delights the palate but also contributes to the bottom line. By the end of this module, you will have a strong foundation in menu management, ready to make informed and strategic decisions in the dynamic world of hotel food and beverage management.
